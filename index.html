What is STEM?

The term 'STEM' is an umbrella term used to group together the distinct but related technical disciplines of Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics. It is a programme intended to prepare learners in the primary and secondary phases of their education for tertiary level should they want to further their studies in the aforementioned fields (Lutkevich, 2022). The outcome of such a programme is learners who are capable of critical thinking, yielding a robust response to various problems emerging in the real world.

Why less people are enrolled in STEM

We believe that these subjects are already difficult. So as difficult as they are, they are not fun to learn. We also believe that it is easier to see how things are done than to read about them. In matric, these subjects are failed probably because there is 80% of theory and 20% practical work. Hence, less people are enrolled to these subjects in higher education due to the fear of failure.



Solutions

  • For science learners: build an interactive lab where students can experiment without getting hurt
  • For Technology learners: have more videos with tasks at the end to do
  • For Engineering learners: Have games whereby they will be building with an application (physical and digital modelling/building).
  • For Maths learners: have quiz games, and possibly integrate mathematics for the above-mentioned fields to better understand the practical applications of maths.
